Problem Statement

Verification and Accountability in Micro-charity

Overview

Charitable giving is powerful for social welfare, but a large portion of society refrains from micro-donations due to a deeply rooted fear: lack of trust, transparency, and accountability. In India, people avoid small cash donations (e.g., to beggars) due to concerns about misuse (drugs, illegal activities) rather than an unwillingness to help. It's an absence of a verifiable mechanism.

"By replacing anonymous cash with digital credits, the project eliminates donor skepticism and ensures that charitable contributions are used solely for verified, essential needs."

Solution Approach

  • Centralized Digital System: Seamless, cash-free micro-donations.
  • Needy Registration: Biometric identity assigned at nearby centers.
  • Donor Registration: Online via Aadhar/ID hooked to payment gateways.
  • Business Integration: Food/Cloth/Med vendors get notified to supply goods.
  • Digital Credits Fulfillment: Needy receive goods directly via their verified accounts.
